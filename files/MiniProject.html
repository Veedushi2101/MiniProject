Ecosystem

An ecosystem refers to a complex structure of the interconnected network, which comprises biotic and also abiotic elements. The Biotic elements include all living organisms which include plants, animals, and also microorganisms. The abiotic elements include non-living beings which are important for survival and include soil, water, and climate, and among the biotic elements, the most important are the flora and fauna.

Flora

Flora is derived from a Latin word, which means “Goddess of the Flower”. Flora is used as a collective term for a group of plantlife that is found in a particular region and the plant kingdom is represented with the help of this name. Adaptation of the plant species is possible and an example of this is Cactus plants which are seen naturally in deserts and have adaptations that modify their characteristics and leaves to preserve water and also protection from predators.

Fauna

According to mythology, Fauna or “Faunus” is the name of a goddess who represents fertility and another meaning is “Fauns” which means “Forest spirits”. Fauna is the name given to collective animal life that was once found in a certain area or time period. In Latin, Fauna is derived from three different sources. Fauna was the name of a Roman goddess representing fertility and the earth, and Faunus was another Roman God.
